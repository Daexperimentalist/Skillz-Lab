Chest

Details

  • general appearance
    • check surrounds
    • pt appearance
      • distress, effortful breathing
      • respiratory rate
      • use of accessory muscles
      • cyanosis
      • sound
        • wheeze
        • stridor
          • rasping or croaking noise loudest on inspiration
          • foreign body
          • tumor
          • epiglottis
          • inflammation
        • spontaneous cough
        • hoarseness
          • laryngitis
          • recurrent laryngeal nerve palsy (e.g. from an apical lung cancer)
          • GERD
  • peripheral signs of lung disease
    • hand
      • clubbing
        • carcinoma of the lung
        • chronic lung suppuration ( pulmonary abscess, tuberculosis, bronchiectasis)
        • interstitial lung disease
      • cigarette staining
      • wasting of the small muscles of the hand
        • peripheral lung tumour of a lower trunk of the brachial plexus
    • wrist arm
      • pulse rate and bp
    • face
      • eyes
        • constricted pupil and a partial ptosis (partial closure of one eyelid) comprise Horner’s syndrome
        • central cyanosis by inspecting the tongue
    • neck
      • trachea
        • check displacement
          • Slight displacement to the right is fairly common in healthy people.
          • significant displacement = upper lobes disease

        • tracheal tug
          • moves down with inspiration
          • sign of gross overexpansion of the chest because of airflow obstruction
  • cough
    • ask pt to cough several times
    • if lacked explosive beginning?

      vocal cord paralysis ( bovine cough)

    • if muffled, wheezy and ineffective?

      COPD

    • if very loose, productive?
      • chronic bronchitis
      • pneumonia or bronchiectasis
    • if dry, irritating cough?
      • infection
      • asthma
      • carcinoma of the bronchus
      • less commonly with LVF or interstitial lung disease
    • check sputum volume, type ( purulent, mucoid or muco-purulent), presence of blood
  • test the forced expiratory time
    • ask pt to take maximum inspiration and then exhale forcefully and completely through open mouth
    • auscultate if can't hear the exhaling
    • count the time of lung emptying
    • normal is 3s or less
    • 9s or more is strongly suggestive of COPD
  • examine the chest 👈

    should be examined anteriorly and posteriorly
